When an ADD system is in recovery, it is easy to attribute every struggle—every moment of "brain fog" or sudden irritability—to damaged hardware or psychological stress. However, from a dietetic perspective, many of these so-called "glitches" are actually biological distress signals indicating a lack of specific raw materials.

You cannot expect a high-performance engine to run smoothly on low-grade fuel. This checklist is designed to help you audit your system, connecting common ADD symptoms to potential micronutrient gaps.

*Disclaimer: This checklist is a tool for data gathering, not self-diagnosis. It is intended to guide dietary adjustments and inform conversations with healthcare professionals.*

---

## **Glitch Category 1: The "Fog" & Initiation Failure**

**The Symptom:** You know exactly what needs to be done (e.g., a technical writing task), but you feel physically unable to start. Your thinking feels slow, heavy, like wading through mud. You stare at the screen in a state of paralysis.

**The Potential Deficiency: The Oxygen & Energy Carriers**

* **Iron (Fe):** Iron is the spark plug for dopamine synthesis (via the enzyme *tyrosine hydroxylase*) and is essential for transporting oxygen to brain tissue. Low iron literally suffocates cognitive processes, making task initiation feel insurmountable.
* *Dietary Check:* Are you consuming enough heme iron (red meat, organ meats) or combining non-heme sources (lentils, spinach) with Vitamin C?


* **Vitamin B12 & Folate (B9):** These are crucial for methylation and nerve health. A deficiency here leads to "macrocytic anemia" of the brain—cognitive sluggishness and poor memory recall.
* *Dietary Check:* Are you getting adequate B12 from animal products or fortified foods? Are dark leafy greens a daily staple for folate?



---

## **Glitch Category 2: Irritability & Sensory Overload**

**The Symptom:** Your fuse is non-existent. You startle easily at loud noises, find physical sensations (like cold or textures) unbearable, and feel a constant low-level hum of anxiety or "bracing" in your body.

**The Potential Deficiency: The System "Coolants"**

* **Magnesium (Mg):** Magnesium is the brain's primary relaxation mineral. It acts as a gatekeeper for NMDA receptors, preventing neurons from over-firing. Without sufficient magnesium, the nervous system is stuck in a hyper-excitable state, leading to tension and high sensory sensitivity.
* *Dietary Check:* Are you regularly consuming high-magnesium foods like pumpkin seeds, almonds, dark chocolate, or leafy greens?


* **Zinc (Zn):** Zinc modulates the brain's stress response and regulates neurotransmitter signaling. A deficiency can leave the system stuck in "fight-or-flight," making you reactive rather than responsive.
* *Dietary Check:* Do your meals include sources like oysters, beef, chickpeas, or cashews?



---

## **Glitch Category 3: The Focus Crash & "Wait-Mode"**

**The Symptom:** You might start the day okay, but by early afternoon, your cognitive battery is dead. You fall into "Wait-Mode," unable to transition to the next task, doom-scrolling while waiting for energy that never returns.

**The Potential Deficiency: The Structural Stabilizers**

* **Vitamin D:** Acting more like a neuro-hormone than a vitamin, Vitamin D is essential for overall brain health, mood regulation, and fighting cognitive fatigue. Low levels are strongly correlated with the "mid-day slump" and depressive moods.
* *Dietary Check:* Do you get daily sun exposure? Do you eat fatty fish or fortified foods? Have you had your levels tested recently?


* **Omega-3 Fatty Acids (EPA/DHA):** These fats make up the cell membranes of your neurons. If you lack Omega-3s, your neuronal membranes become "stiff," making it difficult for dopamine receptors to "dock" and receive signals efficiently.
* *Dietary Check:* Are you consuming fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines) at least twice a week, or taking a high-quality algae/fish oil supplement?

## **Conclusion: The Audit as Empowerment**

By viewing symptoms through a biochemical lens, you transform them from moral failings ("I'm just lazy/angry") into solvable engineering problems ("My system is low on Magnesium").

Use this checklist over a week. If you notice a consistent pattern—for example, daily irritability and sensory overload—prioritize the "Group C" foods from your **Biochemical Grocery Protocol**. When you stabilize your biology with the correct micronutrients, you build a resilient foundation capable of supporting the demands of an ADD mind, even in recovery.
